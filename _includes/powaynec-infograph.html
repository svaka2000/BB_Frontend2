3 Initial Ideas

Our founding design-based research hypotheses

01
Volunteer Onboarding Fast Track
Streamline the volunteer onboarding flow so new PNEC members can find their neighborhood, understand their role, and take their first action in a single visit — no email chains or manual handoffs required.
Problem: The volunteer journey is unclear and fragmented across disconnected pages.
02
Action-First Emergency Navigation
Elevate critical emergency resources to top-level, one-click navigation. Residents should never be more than one tap from shelter info, evacuation routes, or emergency contacts — especially on mobile during a crisis.
Problem: Users struggle to find urgent preparedness actions under the current buried navigation.
03
Standardized Operational Pages
Build consistent, reusable templates for events, drills, and coordinator contacts so the site stays organized as PNEC grows — and non-technical content editors can update pages without breaking layouts.
Problem: Content structure is inconsistent, causing confusion during site updates.

User Management & Login System

We are adapting our existing OCS user management infrastructure and login system to serve PNEC specifically — replacing the generic setup with a PNEC-branded, role-aware portal that gives coordinators, volunteers, and administrators the right access to the right tools.

What We're Adapting
Swap the generic OCS login page for a PNEC-branded portal with the org's identity (colors, logo, mission)
Repurpose existing JWT-based session auth — no rebuilding from scratch, just reconfiguring for PNEC's needs
Connect user accounts to PNEC neighborhood assignments so each login shows relevant local content
Replace OCS-specific dashboard widgets with PNEC-relevant panels (events, drills, coordinator contacts)
Personalization Plans
Volunteers see their assigned neighborhood zone, upcoming drills, and their coordinator on login
Coordinators get a management view to update their neighborhood's event listings and contacts
Admins access a full content dashboard to publish announcements, manage volunteers, and run reports
Public visitors (no login) see all static preparedness info — gating only applies to internal tools
Mailing list signup integrates with Constant Contact so new accounts auto-join the PNEC newsletter
Role Hierarchy
Admin Full site + user management
Neighborhood Coordinator Manage local events & contacts
Volunteer Personalized neighborhood dashboard
Public Visitor Read-only preparedness resources

APCSP Concepts in Action

How core CS principles power this project

Abstraction
Reusable Component Library

Nav, footer, event cards, and resource cards are built once as reusable templates. Add a new event by writing a single YAML entry — not 30 lines of HTML. Complexity is hidden behind clean interfaces.

Algorithms
Search & Filter Logic

A custom JavaScript algorithm iterates over the resources list, applies keyword matching (string comparison) and category selection (conditional logic), then dynamically renders only matching results.

Data & Lists
Structured Content Management

All site content — events, resources, neighborhoods — is stored as structured YAML data files (lists of objects). Jekyll templates iterate over these collections to auto-generate pages.

Impact of Computing
Emergency Preparedness at Scale

Technology makes emergency information accessible to ~70,000 Poway residents. Faster, clearer access during a disaster can directly save lives — the social benefit of purposefully designed software.

Community Impact

PNEC is a volunteer-run nonprofit whose mission depends on fast, clear communication during emergencies. The current site's poor mobile experience and buried navigation mean residents may fail to find critical resources when they need them most. Our redesign removes every unnecessary click between a resident and life-saving information — cutting path length to key actions from 4+ clicks to 1.

By targeting a 90+ Lighthouse score, full mobile compatibility, and WCAG 2.1 accessibility standards, we're ensuring the site works for all Poway residents — including elderly users and those on low-bandwidth mobile connections during an emergency.
